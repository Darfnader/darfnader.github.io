About Me

Born in Sacramento, California, but raised in a small town north of Tulsa, Oklahoma, called Ramona, Dylan Sears is a Phd candidate in sociology with an emphasis on criminology at Kansas State University. He recieved his master's degree in 2022 from Kansas State University in 2022 and continued on to the PhD program. His research encompasses a number of areas, but most often focuses on the relationship between race, class, and crime with an emphasis on racial violence. He has two projects currently in the works, the first examining the celebrity of Kyle Rittenhouse after his trial following the shootings in Kenosha, Wisconsin on August 28th 2020 (for which he won the graduate student paper award from the American Society of Criminology Division on Critical Criminology and Social Justice) and the ongoing gentrification of Tulsa, Oklahoma's Black Wall Street district through the lens of dark tourism and hauntology. He is beginning work on his dissertation that aims to explore the legacies of racial violence in the city of Tulsa, Oklahoma.
